Ingredients

  • Fresh tuna (or any firm white fish) – ≈ 500 g, cut into bite‑size pieces
  • Water – 1 liter (adjust for desired thickness)
  • ½ medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, thinly sliced
  • 1–2 green chilies, slit lengthwise (optional)
  • A few sprigs curry leaves (optional)
  • 1 tbsp lime or lemon juice
  • Salt – to taste
  • Handful fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Method

  1. Prepare the broth base: Bring water to a gentle boil, then add onion, garlic, ginger, chilies, and curry leaves. Simmer 3‑4 min.
  2. Add the fish: Lower heat so the broth stays just below a rolling boil. Slip the fish pieces in and simmer 5‑7 min until opaque.
  3. Season: Salt to taste, finish with lime/lemon juice.
  4. Serve: Ladle over steamed rice, garnish with cilantro, extra lime or fried onions if desired.

Tips for the Best Garudhiya

  • Use the freshest fish you can find – a clear broth starts with fresh ingredients.
  • Keep the heat low after adding fish to avoid clouding the broth.
  • Adjust acidity at the table; many people love a bright, sour finish.
