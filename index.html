Garudhiya – Maldivian Clear Fish Broth

Ingredients

  • 1 kg whole reef‑fish (tuna, snapper, or any firm white fish), cleaned and cut into large pieces
  • 2 – 2½ L water (enough to cover the fish)
  • 1 medium onion, sliced thinly
  • 2 cm ginger, sliced into thin strips
  • 2‑3 green chilies, slit lengthwise (adjust to taste)
  • A handful of fresh curry leaves (optional)
  • Juice of ½ lime (or to taste)
  • Salt, to taste

Method

  1. Prepare the fish: Rinse the pieces under cold water and pat dry.
  2. Start the broth: Bring water to a gentle boil. Add onion, ginger, chilies, and curry leaves. Simmer a couple of minutes.
  3. Add the fish: Lower the fish pieces gently, reduce heat to a low simmer (no rolling boil).
  4. Season: Sprinkle salt. Cook 15‑20 min until the flesh flakes easily. Skim any foam for clarity.
  5. Finish: Turn off heat, stir in lime juice, taste and adjust.
  6. Serve: Ladle broth with a generous piece of fish. Traditionally served with steamed rice, rihaakuru, and fresh sides (cucumber, boiled egg).

Tips & Variations

  • Choose fresh, firm‑white fish for a clean flavor.
  • Skim foam early to keep the broth crystal‑clear.
  • Adjust heat by adding or omitting chilies.
  • Serve with a simple salad of sliced onions, tomatoes, and a drizzle of lemon.

*Feel free to customize the garnish or pair the broth with your favorite side dishes.*
